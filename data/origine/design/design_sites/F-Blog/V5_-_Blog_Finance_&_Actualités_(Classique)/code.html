Global Markets Bracing for Central Bank Pivot as Inflation Cools

The era of aggressive rate hikes may be nearing its conclusion. Analysts across Wall Street and the City of London are recalibrating their models as recent data suggests a faster-than-anticipated slowdown in consumer price growth across the G7 nations. This shift comes at a critical juncture for emerging markets.

While the Fed remains officially hawkish, the undercurrents of the bond market tell a different story. "The market is smelling a pivot," says Elena Rossi, Chief Economist at Vertex Capital. "We are seeing unprecedented positioning in anticipation of the first cut in Q3."

By David H. Sterling
